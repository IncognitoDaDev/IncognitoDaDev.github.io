This is a C++ project made to simulate and solve Rubik Cubes in console. The idea of creating this project was firstly suggested to me by a mentor during a robotics competition named FTC organized by the organizators of FIRST as an attempt to improve my coding capabilities and have something to show during my time in robotics. Two years later of hesistation, I have given it a try.

Rotating the Rubik Cube

Yay, we can now read the rubik, but... how can we play it?
Oh boy... This is now where things get tricky really... fast.

Lets understand our problem by rephrasing the question: how do we make a turn on the rubik?
A turn of the rubik cube implies turning 9 cubies (a rubik cube is made out of 26 cubies where a cubie represents one of smaller cubes) which means turning one of 3x3 matrices by 90 degrees.

But that is not enough since we only turned a face but not its neighboor faces. So we turn our attention to the variable that remembers every face's orientation and switch the colors between the neighboor faces in a roundabout pattern. // Insert here a disected rubik cube Cool! Again, for simplicity, we create a function that asks for a character, if its a anticlockwise turn and how many 90 degrees turn. This same function will determine where our turn will take place depending on the switch cases. Effectively translating the letters into certain turns of our choice. To explain better our problem: if we attempt a B rotation, it will not choose the edges its suppose to turn as every edge is unique to the face that is being turned.

This problem is solved by making a function that tries to reorganize the faces in a way that the face we want to make a turn on is the front face then reverse the orientations back to their original.
